As all the aspects of our lives are being transferred onto apps and websites, it's important for the general public to be literate about how "data tracking" works, and how computers can model human sentiment through their presence online alone. This blog hopes to explain how people's interactions with web technologies can be used to teach very societal and emotional things to computers, and illumate the potential gain and risks from that.

To give some fun context for what I mean, below is a form to test out a model's understanding of emojis, and the context of when to use them. This dataset used to train this neural network consisted of 1.3 billion tweets containing emojis. It can achieve some accurate results, given the sentence is emotionally charged enough. Below are some sample sentences to try (suggested by the Deepmoji Team).

  • I love mom's cooking.
  • My flight is delayed.. amazing
  • I love how you never reply back...
  • I love cruising with my homies
  • I love messing with yo mind!!'
  • This is the shit!
  • This is shit.

This is a reemplementation Bjarke et al's original work at MIT, ported from Python2 to Python3. The original results can be seen here: https://deepmoji.mit.edu/
